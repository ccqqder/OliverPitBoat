## In the deepfake era, what most people lack isn't a camera — it's verifiable authenticity

Lowering the barrier to video and audio production is generally good; but when the cost of fabrication, re-editing, and taking things out of context also drops, "I recorded it" no longer equals "I can prove this is how it really happened." Atomic Presence steps into exactly that gap. It's not a conventional recording app — it's a tool for beginning to build an evidence chain at the moment of recording.

This means its target scenarios aren't casual everyday captures, but more sensitive situations: interviews, witness accounts, whistleblowing, contested scenes, or any moment when you're worried that the material might later be questioned, cut, or forged.

## How it differs from ordinary recording tools

Most tools think about recording the file well first, then about preservation. Atomic Presence instead builds hash chains, dynamic QR codes, and digital signatures into the recording process as it happens. In other words, it doesn't reinforce after the fact — it treats "can this be verified" as the product's core from the beginning.

This makes it more like a technical defense tool for risk scenarios. You may not use it every day, but when you need it, you'll want it to already be on your phone, with a clear enough process that you're not scrambling to cobble together tools in the moment.

## The four protection levels address different risks

The different protection levels in the product aren't about looking impressive — they exist because different use cases need different costs and intensities. Some situations only need to signal "this is being recorded"; others require near-evidence-grade integrity verification. This gradation prevents the tool from operating in only a crude "fully on" or "fully off" mode, and lets it actually fit into real-world workflows.

For journalists, legal professionals, citizen journalists, or anyone who regularly needs to leave a clear record of conversations — this is valuable. Because what they want isn't a camera with more filters; it's a recording tool with fewer ambiguous zones.

## Privacy and offline aren't add-ons — they're part of credibility

If a tool that claims to stand for authenticity relies heavily on external servers for its core data processing, the credibility itself becomes complicated. Atomic Presence keeps critical computation on-device — not just to protect privacy, but to reduce external dependencies in the evidence chain. The less your material passes through third parties, the easier it is to explain later what it went through.

If what you need isn't more convenient filming, but a recording tool with a better chance of convincing others when disputes arise — it's worth getting familiar with Atomic Presence in advance.
